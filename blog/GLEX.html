Pioneering Neuroadaptive Efficiency at GLEX 2025

Global Lunar Exploration Conference • New Delhi • May 2025

Presenting at GLEX 2025 marked a pivotal milestone for KinKinetics. We unveiled our Neuroadaptive Efficiency Paradigm—a first-of-its-kind framework merging neuroeconomics with real-time physiological monitoring. By optimizing both mission outcomes and crew well-being, our approach demonstrates that cognitive resilience is as critical as technical capability in next-generation space exploration.

Our dialogue with ISRO researchers and international space agencies revealed immediate applications for our paradigm: mitigating cognitive fatigue, enhancing decision-making under extreme stress, and sustaining emotional resilience across long-duration missions. These exchanges have initiated formal collaboration pathways to operationalize our research.

A career highlight: sharing the stage with veteran astronauts and agency directors. Their firsthand accounts of psychological challenges in orbit validated our mission. These insights are now directly shaping our paradigm's next iteration—ensuring our solutions address genuine operational needs rather than theoretical concerns.

The connections forged at Yashobhoomi extended far beyond the programme. From space entrepreneurs to established agencies, each conversation reinforced the commercial and scientific viability of astronaut-centric support systems.

Gratitude: This participation was made possible by Women in Aerospace (WIA), whose support enabled us to represent Cardiff on the global stage and advance our commitment to inclusive space innovation.
